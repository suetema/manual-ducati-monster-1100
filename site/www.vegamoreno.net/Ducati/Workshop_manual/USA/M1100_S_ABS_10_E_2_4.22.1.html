Removal of the fuel tank fairings
Operation
Section reference
Remove the seat
Sect. E 3, Removal of the seat
Unscrew the screws (7) and (12) securing the front tank cover (3) but do not remove the cover.
Undo the screws (4) and (11) and remove the RH tank cover (1) and LH tank cover (2) releasing the tabs (A) from the slots (B) in the rear cover (5): recover the nylon washers (10) and the spacers (13).
Remove the front tank cover (3) and recover the spacers (6) and (14).
Unscrew the special screws (9) and remove the rear tank cover (5).
Refitting the fuel tank fairings
Locate the rear cover (5) on the fuel tank and secure it in place with the special screws (9).
Tighten the two special top screws (9) to the specified torque (Sect. C 3, Frame torque settings).
Check that the four bushes (6) are installed on the front tank cover (3) with the larger diameter side facing upwards and the six spacers (14) as shown.
Locate the front tank cover (3) on the fuel tank.
Insert spacers (13) and quick-couplings (15) into the holes inside the right-hand (1) and left-hand tank covers (2).
Refit the right-hand tank cover (1), inserting the tabs (A) in the corresponding slots (B) in the rear cover (5).
Note
Insert the front lugs (C) under the front tank cover (3).
Repeat the above operations to refit the left-hand tank cover (2).
 
Insert the retaining screws (7) and (12) in the front tank cover (3), starting with the front screws.
Insert the screws (11) complete with the nylon washers (10) and screws (4) in the RH tank cover (1) and LH tank cover (2).
Tighten screws (7) around the tank cap to their specified tightening torque (Sect. C 3, Frame torque settings).
Tighten the screws (4) and (11) as well as the special screw (9) to the specified tightening torque (Sect. C 3, Frame torque set­tings).
Tighten the screws (12) to the specified tightening torque (Sect. C 3, Frame torque settings).
