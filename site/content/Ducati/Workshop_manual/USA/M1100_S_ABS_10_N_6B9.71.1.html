Unscrew and remove the short screws (5) and the long screws (15) and (18) securing the crankcase cover (14).
Tap around the edge of the cover with a plastic mallet to detach it from the crankcase half.
Important
The clutch-side crankcase cover (14) can be removed complete with the clutch drum and primary drive gear.
Remove the clutch cover from the crankcase half, taking care to recover the locating dowel (1).
Remove the O-ring (2) located next to the crankcase half oil way.
Disassembly of the clutch cover
Remove the plug (3) and the O-ring (7), the engine oil pressure sensor (9) and its seal (8), the plug (16) and its seal (17) from the crankcase cover.
Remove the circlip (13) and withdraw the shim (12) and the oil sealing ring (11).
The drilled bush (10) is mounted to the cover by a forced interference fit. Remove it using a suitable puller.
Inspect the oil sealing ring (11) and renew it if necessary.
To check the condition of the oil sealing ring (6) installed in the crankcase cover between the clutch drum and the primary drive gear, first remove the latter two components (Sect. N 6.1, Removal of the clutch).
Reassembly of the clutch cover
If the bush has been renewed, seat the new bush (10) in the clutch cover using a suitable drift or a press.
If the oil sealing ring (11) needs to be renewed, fit the new seal into the clutch cover, positioning it so the side without a spring is facing the circlip (13).
Before fitting, check that the edges of the circlip groove show no sign of burrs which might otherwise damage the sealing ring.
Then fit the shim (12) and the circlip (13).
Important
In order for the oil sealing ring (11) to function correctly, it must have an assembly clearance that allows the circlip (13) to rotate.
If it has been removed, install the new oil sealing ring (6) in the clutch cover, with the spring side facing inwards (see figure), and lubricate the seal lips with engine oil.
Fit the plug (3) and the seal (7). Fit the sensor (9) and the seal (8). Fit the plug (16) and the seal (17).
Refitting the clutch cover
Clean and degrease mating surfaces on the clutch-side crankcase cover and crankcase half and ensure that locating bush (1) and the O-ring (2), located in correspondence with the oil way, are installed in the crankcase.
Apply a uniform continuous bead of Ducati liquid gasket onto crankcase half mating surfaces, working around all the holes.
Lubricate the sealing ring (6) with engine oil and fit the bush 88700.5665 onto the primary drive gear so as not to damage the sealing ring while fitting the cover.
Fit the cover assembly (14) to the crankcase half and insert the retaining screws.
Tighten all the screws (5), (15) and (18) to the specified torque (Sect. C 3, Engine torque settings):
